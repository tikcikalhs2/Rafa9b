�� PC Parts Shopping Guide

Whether you're building your first PC or upgrading an old system, this guide will help you pick the right parts without the confusion. Here's what you need to know:

�� 1. CPU (Processor)

�� Recommended: AMD Ryzen Series

  • What It Does: The brain of your computer—handles everything from games to multitasking.

  • Why Ryzen? Ryzen CPUs offer excellent performance for the price, strong multi-core performance, and wide compatibility.

Use Case Recommended CPU
Budget Build Ryzen 5 5600 / 5600G
Mid-Range Gaming Ryzen 5 7600 / 7600X
High-End Gaming Ryzen 7 7800X3D
Streaming & Editing Ryzen 9 7900X / 7950X

✅ Tip: Look for CPUs ending in "X" for extra performance, or "G" for built-in graphics (no GPU needed).

�� 2. Motherboard (Mainboard)

  • What It Does: Connects all your components.

  • Pick Based On Your CPU:

    • Ryzen 5000 Series → B550 or X570 chipset

    • Ryzen 7000 Series → B650 or X670 chipset (Note: Uses new AM5 socket + DDR5 RAM)

Feature B-Series (e.g., B550/B650) X-Series (e.g., X570/X670)
Budget-Friendly ✅ ❌ More Expensive
Overclocking Support ✅ ✅
PCIe 4.0/5.0 Support ✅ ✅ (More lanes)

�� 3. RAM (Memory)

  • What It Does: Lets your computer run multiple tasks smoothly.

  • Recommended:

    • Ryzen 5000 → DDR4, 16GB–32GB, 3200MHz or higher

    • Ryzen 7000 → DDR5, 6000MHz sweet spot

✅ Tip: Dual-channel (2 sticks) is better than a single stick.

�� 4. GPU (Graphics Card)

  • What It Does: Renders images, video, and games.

  • Pairing With Ryzen:

    • Ryzen 5 5600G → No GPU needed (has integrated graphics)

    • Ryzen 5 7600 and up → Needs a dedicated GPU

Gaming Level Suggested GPUs
1080p RX 6600, RTX 3060
1440p RX 6700 XT, RTX 4060 Ti
4K RX 7900 XTX, RTX 4080/4090

⚡ 5. Power Supply (PSU)

  • What It Does: Powers your system.

  • What You Need:

    • Look for 80+ Bronze or better

    • Wattage:

      • Basic build: 550W–650W

      • High-end GPU: 750W+

✅ Tip: Modular PSUs help with cable management.

�� 6. Cooling

  • Ryzen CPUs come with stock coolers (like the Wraith Stealth/Spire), but for X or high-end models, consider:

    • �� Air Coolers: Noctua NH-U12S, be quiet! Pure Rock 2

    • �� AIO Liquid Coolers: 240mm+ for Ryzen 7/9

�� 7. Storage

  • Types:

    • SSD (Fast) → For OS & games

    • HDD (Large capacity, slower) → For storage

Type Use
NVMe SSD Fast boot & loading
SATA SSD Budget SSD option
HDD Mass storage (media etc.)

��️ 8. Case (Chassis)

  • Pick Based On:

    • Size: ATX / mATX / ITX

    • Airflow: Mesh front is better

    • GPU clearance & cable management space

✅ Tip: Double-check GPU length & CPU cooler height.

Final Checklist ✅

Before you buy:

  • CPU and motherboard socket match (e.g., AM4 or AM5)

  • RAM is compatible (DDR4 vs DDR5)

  • PSU has enough wattage for your GPU

  • Your case fits everything
